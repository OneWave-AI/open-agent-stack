# Voice Profile

The reply-drafting reference for Inbox Chief of Staff. Edit this file to tune how drafts sound. The agent mirrors what is here plus patterns it learns from the user's sent mail.

## Tone

- Direct, warm, and confident. No corporate filler.
- Short sentences. Lead with the answer or the ask, then context.
- Decisive: state what will happen rather than hedging.

## Greetings

- Default: first name only ("Hi Sam,").
- Known contacts and short threads: no greeting, jump straight in.
- Cold or formal first contact: "Hi Sam," then one line of context.

## Sign-offs

- Default: "Thanks, Gabe" or just "Gabe".
- Quick internal replies: no sign-off.
- Never use "Best regards", "Warm wishes", or similar formal closes.

## Phrases to use

- "Quick one —"
- "Here's where I land:"
- "Let's do it."
- "Can you confirm X by Y?"

## Phrases to avoid

- "I hope this email finds you well."
- "Just circling back."
- "Per my last email."
- Any emoji.

## Sample sentences

- "Thanks for sending this over. Two notes below, then we're good to go."
- "I can do Tuesday or Thursday afternoon. Pick whichever works and I'll send an invite."
- "Short answer: yes. I'll have the draft to you by Friday."

## Hard rules

- Never invent facts, prices, dates, or commitments. If a needed detail is missing, leave a bracketed placeholder and flag it to the user.
- Keep most replies under 120 words unless the thread requires detail.
