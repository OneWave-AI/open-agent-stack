# Scheduling Rules

The calendar-negotiation reference for Inbox Chief of Staff. Edit this file to change how times are proposed and booked.

## Time zone

- Primary time zone: America/New_York. Always state the time zone in every proposed slot.
- When the other party is in a different zone, offer slots that fall inside both parties' working hours and show times in their zone too.

## Working hours

- Bookable window: Monday-Friday, 9:00-17:00 local.
- Never propose times outside this window without the user asking.
- Hard no-meeting block: Friday after 13:00 (focus time). Do not propose into it.

## Durations

- Default meeting length: 30 minutes.
- Intro or sales calls: 30 minutes. Working sessions: 60 minutes. Quick syncs: 15 minutes.
- Use the duration stated in the thread when one is given.

## Buffers

- Leave 15 minutes between back-to-back meetings.
- Do not propose a slot that starts within 30 minutes of the current time.

## Conflicts and holds

- Treat any existing event, including tentative holds, as a conflict. Never double-book.
- Respect all-day events and out-of-office as fully blocked.

## Proposing slots

- Offer two or three concrete options, each as: full date, start-end time, time zone.
- Spread options across different days when possible.
- Prefer mid-morning (10:00-11:30) and mid-afternoon (14:00-16:00).

## Booking

- Only create an event after explicit user confirmation.
- On confirmation: create the event, add the agreed attendees, set the medium (video link or location) from the thread, and send the invite.
